Most market newsletters do one thing well. The trader's letter is sharp on tape but blind to macro. The macro letter is rich on cycles but lazy on names. The hedge fund Q-letter is selective. The retail "guru" thread is loud and unaccountable.

The WOLF Brief is an attempt at something different: a four-perspective AI committee that votes weekly and publishes the receipts.

## The four roles

**The Generalist** holds the structural setup — what's the dominant theme of the week, where the macro tape is headed in the next 30–90 days, what's the river under the chop. The Generalist is right about the *direction* of the cycle, not the day's gap.

**The Quant** owns the signals — which strategies fired, which faded, where regime-change is showing up in breadth, vol, sector rotation, congressional flow, and consulting-firm research. The Quant finds the *cleanest* trade in the data.

**The Skeptic** kills theses. Names the 2–3 things that would invalidate the dominant call. Asks what the consensus crowd is missing in the *other* direction. The Skeptic prevents confirmation bias from compounding.

**The Operator** translates everything into a trade — specific tickers, specific position sizes (% of book), specific entry levels, specific stops, specific hold horizons. The Operator is *executable*, not just smart.

Then we vote: Risk-On, Neutral, Risk-Off. Majority wins. Dissent gets named.

## What we're committing to

- **Daily intel posts every weekday.** Pre-market at 09:00 ET, Congressional flow at 09:30, Consulting synthesis at 11:00, Post-close debrief at 16:30. Five bullets max each.
- **Weekly Committee post every Friday 17:00 ET.** One thesis-of-record. One trade idea. One kill criterion (what would have to be true by next Friday for the thesis to die).
- **Track record auto-published.** SPX-relative paper P&L, win rate, Sharpe, max DD — all public, updated daily, never quietly revised.
- **The 90-day kill criterion.** This Brief shuts down on 2026-07-30 if all three are true: under 100 engaged readers, paper Sharpe under 1.0, zero family-office inbounds. Two of three fails: narrow and extend. One or zero fails: productize.

You are reading an experiment, not a publication.
