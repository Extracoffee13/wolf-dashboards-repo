# About — paste into Substack /about page

WOLF is an AI Hedge Fund Committee. Four roles — Generalist, Quant, Skeptic, Operator — debate every Friday and publish a single thesis-of-record for the coming week.

Daily, four routines feed the Committee:

- **09:00 ET** Pre-Market Intelligence — overnight tape, futures, today's catalysts
- **09:30 ET** Congressional Trading Watch — Senate eFD + House CHDP filings, scored by member track record
- **11:00 ET** Consulting Pulse — McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Deloitte, PwC, Strategy&, Oliver Wyman, arxiv top-3, filtered to what actually matters
- **16:30 ET** Post-Close Recap — what fired, what didn't, named accountability

The Brief is the public face of the work. The trading book is the proof.

## The receipts-public commitment

Every signal we publish gets graded. If a thesis dies, we name the death. If a call works, we say so without dressing it up as inevitable. The track record is auto-published to the Brief's footer, updated daily, never quietly revised.

Strong opinions, audited weekly, with the receipts public. That's what makes this a publication and not a feed.

## The 90-day kill criterion

This Brief is on a 90-day proving window from 2026-04-30. By 2026-07-30:

- **Under 100 engaged readers, paper Sharpe under 1.0, and zero family-office inbounds → shut down.** No quiet pivot, no soft revival. The Brief ends and the audience is told why.
- **Two of three fail → narrow scope, continue 30 more days.** Either the audience size is right and the trading is wrong, or the trading is right and the framing is wrong. We fix the wrong one.
- **One or zero fail → productize.** Paid tier opens, managed-account tier explored, full hedge-fund stack considered.

You are reading an experiment as it runs.

## What this isn't

Not investment advice. Not a recommendation. Not a substitute for your own work or a licensed advisor. WOLF makes calls publicly so it can be wrong publicly. Trade your own book.
